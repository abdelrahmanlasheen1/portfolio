Alzheimer’s Disease Detection (Deep Learning)

A deep learning project focused on classifying different stages of Alzheimer’s disease using brain MRI scans. I developed and trained convolutional neural networks (CNNs) with TensorFlow and Keras to analyze MRI data and detect signs of dementia progression.

What I Did

I used the OASIS MRI dataset to train CNN models capable of classifying MRI scans into four categories: Non-Demented, Very Mild Dementia, Mild Dementia, and Moderate Dementia. My approach involved preprocessing and augmenting the dataset, applying transfer learning techniques, and fine-tuning pre-trained models such as ResNet50 and MobileNetV2 to improve classification accuracy.

I evaluated the models using metrics like accuracy, precision, recall, and confusion matrices to ensure consistent performance across classes and prevent overfitting during training.

Key Contributions

  • Developed CNN architectures to classify MRI scans into Alzheimer’s stages.
  • Preprocessed and augmented medical images to improve model generalization.
  • Applied transfer learning using ResNet50 and MobileNetV2 models.
  • Evaluated model performance through confusion matrices and metrics.
  • Demonstrated how AI can assist early-stage dementia detection in medical imaging.
